# Q Weather USA Pro

Live US weather and severe-weather alerts on your Das Keyboard Q, powered
by the official National Weather Service.

## What you see on the keyboard

Four keys light up — one per upcoming day — color-coded to the forecast:

| Color            | Weather                                                     |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Yellow           | Clear or sunny                                              |
| Purple           | Cloudy, overcast, or fog                                    |
| Blue             | Rain, showers, or drizzle                                   |
| Red              | Thunderstorm or hail                                        |
| White            | Snow, sleet, or blizzard                                    |
| **Flashing red** | **Active severe-weather alert** for your location           |

When a severe-weather alert is active for your area, the leftmost key
flashes red and the other three shift right to show the next three days.

**Hover** any key to see the detailed forecast for that day.
**Click** any key to open the official NWS forecast page in your browser.

## Setup

1. Install the applet from the Q marketplace.
2. Drag it onto the four keys you want to use (for example, the top-left
   corner of your keyboard).
3. In the applet settings, type your city name into the **Search a US city**
   box and pick your location from the list.

Examples that work:

- `Charlotte` → Charlotte, North Carolina
- `Austin TX`
- `Seattle`
- `Honolulu`

The applet refreshes every 30 minutes (the rate the NWS recommends).

## United States only

This applet uses the US National Weather Service, so it only works for
US locations. International cities will not appear in the search.

## Privacy

- No account, no sign-up, no API keys.
- The applet sends your selected city's latitude and longitude to two
  free public services: the National Weather Service (forecast and
  alerts) and Open-Meteo (city name search).
- No personal data is collected or transmitted.
